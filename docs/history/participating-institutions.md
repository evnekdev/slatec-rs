# Participating institutions and people

## Scope

This page records institutions and named representatives explicitly identified in the July 1993 SLATEC Guide. It separates founding participation, later membership, 1993 representation, and wider routine authorship. Historical names are retained; current institutional successors should not be substituted without evidence.

## Broader contributor community

The voting subcommittee should not be confused with the complete set of algorithm authors. SLATEC integrated routines from established packages and from individual contributors, and the prescribed prologue retained author names, affiliations, references, and sublibrary identity. The current source index includes authors associated with Sandia, LANL, NBS/NIST, Bell Laboratories, Jet Propulsion Laboratory, and other organizations ([`slatec-guide`](https://www.netlib.org/slatec/guide), Section 8; [`slatec-source-tree`](https://www.netlib.org/slatec/src/)).

**Project implication:** Institutional provenance should be represented with separate fields for the routine author, affiliation at the time recorded, original package, SLATEC adopting body, and present host. Collapsing all of these into “author: SLATEC” would discard important history.

## Institutional naming cautions

- Use **National Bureau of Standards (NBS)** for records written under that name and **National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)** where the source uses NIST.
- Preserve **Air Force Weapons Laboratory** in the 1974 founding account and **Air Force Phillips Laboratory, Kirtland** in the 1993 membership record.
- Preserve the historical LLNL center names in source-derived metadata; do not retroactively rewrite NMFECC as NERSC unless the relationship is explained.
- Treat abbreviations in routine prologues such as `SNLA`, `SNL`, `NBS`, and `LANL` as source strings until normalized against a controlled historical institution table.

## Unresolved questions

- Who served on the subcommittee before the July 1993 membership list, and over what terms?
- What formal relationship existed between the parent technical exchange committee and each participating federal agency?
- Are meeting minutes, ballots, or contributor rosters preserved at Sandia, LANL, LLNL, NIST, ORNL, the Air Force, or OSTI?
- Which institutional abbreviation variants appear across all routine prologues, and which can be normalized without ambiguity?
- Which routines were authored outside participating institutions but adopted through an imported package?
